War & Combat Mechanics (Quick Guide)

Combat has been updated: morale loss and damage are now calculated per-unit and scaled proportionally to opposing forces, so a matched fight will not wipe morale instantly.

Supplies

Every attack consumes supplies. Each unit type has an attack supply cost (shown on the military page). You need at least 200 supplies to start an attack. Supplies regenerate over time for both sides and are capped per war.

How Morale Loss Is Calculated

Instead of a flat morale hit, the game computes a morale impact using three factors:

  • Unit weight: each unit type contributes a small weight (e.g. tanks, aircraft count more than infantry).
  • Relative strength: attacker vs defender strength is computed from unit counts × weights. The stronger side gains an advantage factor.
  • Win intensity: the fight's win_type scales the final delta (how decisive the win was).

These are combined into a per-attack morale delta for the losing side. Deltas are scaled down and capped so wars require multiple engagements to finish a nation.

Damage & Loot

Casualties are applied proportionally to the units involved. If the war type is Loot and the attacker wins, they may seize up to 10% of the defender's gold (a random integer between 0 and floor(10%)).

Tips

  • Bring a balanced force: counters reduce effective damage from specific unit types.
  • Don't attack without enough supplies — attacks require a minimum pool and will fail if supplies run out.
  • Spy and scout to reveal default defenses before committing large forces.
