How tax income is calculated

Tax income is derived from population, productivity of provinces, and consumer goods availability. Productivity and upgrades multiply base per-capita tax; consumer goods increase the effective tax per person when in supply.

Maximizing income

  • Invest in productivity-related infrastructure (schools, factories) to raise per-capita taxes.
  • Ensure consumer goods supply is sufficient for population to avoid lower tax yields.
  • Use the market to sell low-value surplus resources for quick gold.
